My Teaching Philosophy

As a pre-service teacher, I want a classroom where hands lead the mind, where shaping timber, kneading dough, or wiring a circuit helps students discover who they are and what they can do. Learning begins with trust and safety, and grows through honest effort, mistakes, and reflection. My role is to hold the space: clear routines, explicit safety, real choice, and gentle challenge. I hope every learner leaves with skilled hands, a steadier voice, and the quiet pride that comes from making something that matters.

Core Beliefs

Authentic, Hands-On Learning

Real-world briefs and making—linking theory, industry, and community.

Inclusive & High Expectations

UDL-informed scaffolds, strong routines, and safety underpin success.

Student Agency & Wellbeing

Voice and choice, reflective practice, and relationships drive engagement.
